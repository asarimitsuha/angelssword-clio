[HEADING]CHAPTER 2
Will of the King
Fontina stood alone in the quiet corridor of the airship, the soft glow of lanterns casting elongated shadows on the polished wooden walls. An unsettling feeling gnawed at her, a weight pressing heavily on her chest. She glanced back at the closed door of Princess Cherry's room, the ornate carvings on the door seeming more imposing in the dim light.
Her gaze lingered, concern etched into her features. The steady hum of the ship's engines filled the silence, a constant reminder of their hasty departure. Lowering her head, she raised a hand and bit her thumbnail—a rare lapse in her composed demeanor. The subtle gesture betrayed the storm of doubts swirling within her.
Kaede emerged from Princess Cherry's room, leaving Helene to stand guard as the lamia had suggested they take turns resting. Spotting Fontina in the corridor, Kaede approached quietly. With a gentle nudge on Fontina's cheek, she sought to draw her out of her reverie, her tail flicking left and right.
"You're practically inviting someone to sneak up on you," Kaede teased softly. "What's got you so deep in thought, Fontina?"
Fontina turned to face Kaede, her eyes clouded with worry. The lantern light cast shadows that accentuated the furrow in her brow. "I think that creature was after the Princess," she whispered, her voice barely audible. "But... why? What was that thing? The amount of turmoil this is going to cause..."
She pressed a hand to her chest, feeling the rapid beat of her heart beneath her fingertips. Questions swirled in her mind like a gathering storm, each one without a clear answer. The hum of the airship enveloped them, but it couldn't drown out the unsettling thoughts that gripped her.
Her face grew pale as the weight of realization settled upon her. "What if they think we had something to do with it?" she murmured anxiously. "We fled... but surely they wouldn't suspect us. Cherry is just a child..."
Her eyes narrowed in contemplation, thoughts racing behind them. It was clear that Fontina's role as Princess Cherry's advisor and the governor of Madeline was no coincidence. Her sharp intuition cut through the haze of confusion. The implications of this incident could spiral beyond their control if not handled carefully.
A door nearby opened as Lanes emerged from his cabin, pushing back his hood with one hand while he finished off a glass of water. "This is only going to get worse," he remarked with a weary confidence, glancing toward the princess's door.  He placed the glass on the table with a thud, "Out of all that chaos, it looked like only two members of the royal family survived without at least major injuries. And I doubt the other one is going to be pleased about someone else being next in line."
Fontina nodded slowly, her unease growing. "This makes me uneasy..."
Yasha joined the group, descending from the stairs that led to the crew deck above. She’d been speaking with the captain about their arrival time to Mothergreen and he’d agreed to get there as quickly as he dared. He’d made a grim joke about being several days ahead of schedule as he had been chartered for their return journey as well; the attack had interrupted the multiple days of political meetings that would have followed the wedding.
“We need to face the obvious,” she said, stepping seamlessly into the conversation. “Today’s events were no accident.”
She began pacing thoughtfully. "Let's review what we know. Someone summoned those monsters, likely aiming to eliminate the royal family. I wouldn't be surprised if Prince Casimir tries to blame this on us." She gave Fontina a meaningful look.
Limone rubbed her temples, a mix of worry and disbelief on her face. "But Prince Casimir almost died too!" she exclaimed. "He's gotta be really mad...!"
"I agree with Yasha," Kaede said thoughtfully. "With only Princess Cherry and Casimir left in the royal family, he might pin the blame on Princess Cherry—especially considering how he acted at the wedding. It wouldn't be beyond him."
Lanes leaned against the wall, idly twirling a knife between his fingers. "Casimir was the only one directly 'saved' from death," he mused. "Meanwhile, the others were killed almost instantly. He has a powerful spellcaster at his side; it's suspicious. He might use this tragedy to eliminate other potential heirs."
"Surviving such a catastrophe and losing so much family would earn him sympathy and support from those unaware of his true nature," he added.
Yasha raised an eyebrow. "While I wouldn't put it past him to benefit from this, would he really risk his life in such a gamble? Remember how quickly it happened—if the monster had decided to smash him instead of grabbing him, he'd be dead. He could have stayed further away or left the area. His fear seemed genuine, though it could have been faked."
Limone looked puzzled. "And wasn't he offering to help the Princess earlier? Why would he say that if he didn't mean it?"
“He may not see Princess Cherry as a threat. Trying to play nice before the tragedy might make us more likely to trust him,” Yasha speculated.
Fontina nodded slowly. "Princess Cherry doesn't outrank Prince Casimir in succession... Maybe we're worrying over nothing. As long as we stay loyal, there shouldn't be a problem, right?”

Lanes looked at Limone, then addressed the group. "When Casimir spoke to the Princess earlier, he was showing off—gloating even. Do you think he's the type to tolerate any 'risk' factors? She's loyal now, but what if that changes as she grows older? If she gains popularity and support, she could become a threat. Easier to remove a threat before it grows."
Limone looked down, conflicted. "Yeah... maybe you're right, Lanes."
Yasha also conceded the point with a nod. “Though some things don’t add up, he’s still the one most likely responsible for what happened.”
Kaede gently placed a reassuring hand on Fontina's arm. "I know this is overwhelming, but we need you, Fontina. Your calm thinking and logic can help us piece everything together. We have to consider all possible scenarios."
Fontina took a deep breath, her resolve strengthening. "Whatever's happening, our main goal is to get to Mothergreen and then back to Madeline." She turned to Kaede with a weary smile. "We'll make sure to mark the quest as complete with Angel's Sword so you can get your payment. Thank you for your service, Kaede. I'm sorry we dragged you into this. I thought it would be a simple, enjoyable assignment. I'll have to apologize to your mother later."
Kaede smiled softly. "I thought you'd say that, Fontina." She sighed, then playfully pinched Fontina's cheek. Standing straight, she declared, "I'm staying. There is something else I need to look into, I have some suspicions of the mage in regards to something I'm already involved with; that and I can't leave knowing you all might be in danger. My mother wouldn't approve either."
She met Fontina's gaze firmly. "You need help, and who better than someone you know and trust?"
Fontina exhaled, a genuine smile breaking through. "Ah... I feel better knowing you're with us. We can use all the help we can get. We'll make sure to compensate you—maybe even more than Limone," she teased.
"Wait, what?!" Limone exclaimed, throwing her fists up in mock protest.
Fontina chuckled, easing the tension momentarily.
"The tart tax is harsh, Limone," Lanes quipped with a grin.
Yasha refocused the group. "If anyone else is alive, we’ll know soon enough. We can make more concrete plans once we know more about what the situation is.”
Kaede nodded, "There's no point in excessive speculation, but we should consider worst-case scenarios to be prepared."
She turned to Fontina. "Which is your cue to enlighten us, right?"
Fontina nodded, turning serious. "Worst-case scenario: someone survives and blames us for fleeing so quickly."
She hesitated. "Best-case scenario: someone assumes the throne, and we pledge our loyalty." Her voice wavered slightly.
Yasha pondered, "We have many witnesses who saw us escaping the creatures. But what if both Prince Edmont and Prince Casimir survived and both claim the throne?"
Fontina considered. "Prince Edmont is older and should have the rightful claim. Casimir is senior only to Princess Cherry."
"A potential power struggle," Lanes mused. "Maybe even civil war. They might seek Princess Cherry's support. The one who doubts they'd get it might try to remove her from the picture entirely."
"Normally, the elder inherits," Yasha agreed. "But if one of them orchestrated this tragedy, whoever we support could be seen as the legitimate ruler."
"Exactly," Lanes said. "Her support is powerful—but dangerous."
"Perhaps we're getting ahead of ourselves," Yasha interjected. "Prince Edmont could very well be... gone."
***Meanwhile in Princess Cherry’s cabin:
Princess Cherry stirred beneath the soft blankets, her eyes fluttering open. The gentle hum of the airship's engines filled the room. Reaching out instinctively, her small hand found Helene's. "Helene? What... happened? Cousin Renard...?" she asked softly, her voice laced with confusion and lingering fatigue.
[IMAGE]wotk_ch2_image3.png

Helene offered a comforting smile, her ungloved hand gently enveloping the princess's. "We're safe now, dear one," she reassured. "Here, let's help you sit up a bit. Would you like some tea?" She adjusted the pillows behind Cherry, ensuring she was comfortable. The question about tea was as much to soothe as it was to gauge how alert the young princess was.
Once Cherry was settled with a warm cup cradled in her hands, Helene took a steadying breath. "There's something I need to tell you," she began gently.
She recounted the events that had transpired—the sudden attack at the wedding, the monstrous creature that had appeared, and the ensuing chaos. Helene spoke softly, her words carefully chosen to convey the seriousness without overwhelming the young girl. She explained how they had escaped, the uncertain fates of the other members of the royal family, and their urgent departure from Macaron.
Princess Cherry listened quietly, her eyes reflecting a mix of shock and sorrow. "So many... gone?" she whispered, her grip tightening around the teacup.
Helene nodded solemnly. "I'm afraid so. But we're doing everything we can to ensure your safety."
Cherry looked down at the swirling liquid in her cup. "What will happen to the kingdom?" she asked, her voice barely audible.
"That's something we're all working to figure out," Helene replied gently. "But you don't need to carry that burden alone. We're here to support you."
A tear slipped down Cherry's cheek. "I just... I can't believe it. Cousin Renard was always so kind to me."
Helene reached out, wiping the tear away with a tender touch. "I know, dear one. He was beloved by many."
“We’re on our way to Mothergreen now, and then back home,” Helene continued softly, her eyes gentle as she observed Princess Cherry. She hesitated for a moment. “I'm sure Fontina and Yasha are already working on a solid plan. Would you like me to call everyone in? Or do you feel up to joining them?”
Princess Cherry sat up slowly, her movements deliberate as she processed the weight of recent events. A fleeting shadow passed over her face, but it was quickly replaced by a resolute expression. Nodding to Helene, she stepped out of her private chamber with newfound determination. The rest of their group was gathered in a heavy silence, contemplation hanging between them. As Helene took up position beside Princess Cherry, the rest came to informal attention, their normal state when they were in private with the princess.
"Uncle is dead. No news on the others?" she asked, her voice steady despite the gravity of her words. She paused thoughtfully. "We should buy a newspaper in Mothergreen. It might provide more information and help us form a plan. Right, Fontina?"
Fontina looked up, momentarily startled out of her thoughts. "Yes, Princess," she replied, her tone respectful yet strained.
Princess Cherry then turned to Kaede, her eyes softening. "I thank you on behalf of myself and everyone here for staying with us through this. I will ensure you are properly compensated."
Kaede gave a modest bow, rubbing the back of her head sheepishly. "I'll keep doing my best and offer any insights I can, Princess." She hesitated for a moment before adding, "If it's not too rude, may I speak less formally around you? I'm not the best with all the official etiquette, especially in private settings like this."
She offered a tentative smile, her fox-like ears twitching slightly as she recalled the formalities drilled into her by her guild.
Princess Cherry's lips curved into a gentle smile. "Of course, Kaede. In private, please feel free to be yourself."
Helene felt a swell of pride as she watched the young princess. Despite the turmoil, Cherry was showing the grace and leadership befitting her lineage. Silently, Helene offered a prayer to the old gods, seeking protection and justice for those lost.
The airship gently rocked as it settled into the bustling docks of the Free Trade City of Mothergreen. The massive sails folded gracefully as the hum of the engines quieted, which made it all the more obvious how many airships were docked from the western direction.  A hub of trade yes, but many ships seemed to come from where they did.  Far below things seemed fairly normal, with the exception of increased guard presence.
The ship’s captain returned, wiping sweat from his brow, and tossed a folded newspaper onto the nearest table. His weathered face bore a mix of fatigue and concern. "Got the paper for you as requested," he said gruffly to Yasha before turning to the others. "You're all welcome to stay aboard tonight. We'll need time to refuel and restock, and our next charter isn't for another two days. But be cautious out there in the city—something feels... off. Tensions have been high since the terrorist attack. Stick together and don't wander too far."
With a nod, he headed back to the deck, leaving the group to digest his words. The hum of the city outside contrasted sharply with the quiet unease settling among them.
Yasha unfolded the newspaper, her eyes scanning the headlines. “Dacquoise in Mourning: Calamity Fiend Slaughters Royal Family,” she said as she began to read the paper out loud. It was something of a fluff piece but it did have some of the specifics they had been wondering about on the flight. Confirmation that King Aelric and Prince Renard were dead along with Lady Amelie, Prince Edmont, and Princess Elowen. Prince Casimir was confirmed alive and there was wild speculation about impending war and Northi interference.
“I’m not sure what to make of this,” she said, setting the paper on the table. “Aside from the obvious misinformation about military strikes, there’s no mention of Princess Cherry. Is the writer simply incompetent or is there more at play here?”
Princess Cherry glanced at Fontina, sensing her advisor's rising tension. Despite her own feelings, she managed a small, reassuring smile. "Perhaps I'm not considered a significant member of the family," she said quietly, her voice tinged with a hint of sadness. Her fingers traced the edge of the table absently, as if trying to ground herself amidst the swirling uncertainty.
Limone snatched up the paper, her eyes wide as she skimmed the articles. "This is crazy! A military strike? What?!" she exclaimed, disbelief evident in her tone. She flipped through the pages rapidly, as if expecting the next page to contradict the last.
Fontina's face had gone pale, her usual composure slipping. She clutched her fan tightly, knuckles white. "This is... worse than I feared," she admitted softly. "I was hoping someone else had survived." Her voice wavered, the weight of their situation pressing heavily upon her.
Cherry's gaze softened further as she watched Fontina. The loss of so many and the implications of the news were overwhelming.
Kaede's ears drooped slightly as she leaned over Limone's shoulder to read. "I'm not sure what to make of this either," she murmured. "It doesn't make sense to leave Princess Cherry out."
She looked up, concern etched across her features. "Regardless, the moment Princess Cherry is recognized, something is bound to happen—whether news spreads or something else entirely."
Helene frowned. "No, an event of this magnitude... There's no way even the most distant nobles would be left unmentioned," she said thoughtfully. "And while she may be far from the immediate line of succession, Princess Cherry is still a legitimate heir. If this isn't a deliberate attempt to erase her from public consideration, I don't know what it is."
She gestured to the paper in Limone’s hands. "It specifically states that Prince Edmont and Princess Elowen were among the dead, but given how this reads like a puff piece promoting Prince Casimir's claim to the throne, I wouldn't trust it without our own investigation."
"It's not a good sign when 'no civil war' is the worst scenario," Lanes mused, his eyes flickering with thought. "And with Edmont found dead... it's possible that in all the confusion, Casimir took an opportunity. Even if he wasn't the primary orchestrator, the motive and opportunity were there." He folded the paper thoughtfully, his expression darkening as he considered the implications.
Fontina's brow furrowed deeply as she gazed out toward the bustling docks, her mind racing with unspoken concerns. The distant sounds of the harbor—the creaking of ships, the calls of sailors—did little to soothe her unease. She glanced at Kaede, who seemed equally lost in thought, before murmuring under her breath, "It's also a good thing we didn't make it obvious which charter we were using, or we'd be swamped right about now..."
Limone's eyes widened as she began counting on her fingers, her lips moving silently as she processed the situation. Suddenly, she blurted out, "Man... Prince Casimir is gonna be the king?!" Her voice was a mix of disbelief and dawning realization, echoing the unspoken dread that hung over the group.
"Unfortunately, yes," Yasha replied, her tone measured yet edged with frustration. "Unless we want to publicly accuse him of causing this disaster..." She let the suggestion hang in the air, knowing full well the peril such an accusation would entail.
Princess Cherry leaned back, her fingers drumming lightly on the table. Her tone was light, almost teasing, but her words carried a sharp edge. "We could always secede to Queen Renia..." she quipped, a wry smile tugging at her lips. It was clear she was far from pleased with the possible future unfolding before them.
Helene winced at the suggestion, offering Princess Cherry a strained smile. "Well, there's a reason Nuren pledged itself to Dacquoise, Princess," she said gently, her eyes reflecting both sympathy and caution.
Kaede sighed heavily, leaning against the wooden wall as she tried to puzzle things together. "If we accuse him and it backfires massively, we'll be running for our lives indefinitely," she thought aloud. Her ears drooped slightly, mirroring her uncertainty. "We need to consider our moves carefully."
Fontina suddenly moved purposefully toward a sturdy chest secured in the corner of the cabin. With a soft click, she unlocked it and retrieved a small bag of coins. The weight of it felt insubstantial in her hand—a stark reminder of their dwindling resources. She handed it to Yasha, her expression serious and resolute. "This is all the funds we have left after settling our expenses," she informed her. "It's absolutely everything until we get back to Madeline. Can you handle resupplying while we wait here?"
Yasha accepted the bag with a nod, the coins clinking softly. "What do we need?" she asked, her gaze steady as she prepared to take stock of their necessities.
Before anyone could respond, Limone's hand shot up enthusiastically. Her eyes sparkled with excitement as she exclaimed, "Meat!" A wide grin spread across her face, momentarily lightening the somber mood.
Yasha sighed softly, a hint of amusement breaking through her stern exterior. "Food for the road, noted," she replied. "Anything else?" Her gaze swept over the group, lingering on Fontina for further instructions.
Fontina tapped her chin thoughtfully, her mind already calculating their next steps. "We should probably... buy a new wagon," she mused aloud, glancing around at her companions. "What do you all think?"
"I agree," Kaede chimed in, nodding decisively. "Getting a new wagon will draw less attention. And maybe we could sell the old one to make some clim back?" She looked to Fontina for approval, her tail swishing slightly as she considered the practicalities.
Yasha shook her head, her expression firm. "Selling our old wagon at this point will only draw more attention," she objected. "We need to be out of the city before anyone realizes it's missing. Leaving it here leaves a mystery—selling it could leave a trail."
"That's my concern as well," Helene added, her gaze thoughtful. "Selling the old wagon creates a paper trail, implying deliberate action on our part. Leaving it behind suggests we never arrived in Mothergreen at all. I don't like the implications that could arise from selling it."
"I think considering the old wagon a loss is the safest play if we want to remain inconspicuous," Lanes agreed, crossing his arms. "Better to avoid any unnecessary risks."
Plans began to come together as the group began to discuss the details of what was needed for the trip home. The amount of provisions and types (“Meat!” Limone was quick to remind at every opportunity) and where to get them. Helene produced a map to plot out travel time and camp sites to best avoid towns and known monster concentrations. As more and more details began to come together, Kaede pulled aside and motioned to Yasha.
“Can I speak with you for a moment, Yasha?”  Kaede motioned.Nodding in curiosity, the Oni followed after, “Yeah what is it, Kaede?”
Kaede spoke, “There’s something else I should mention… that mage who intervened against the creature and saved Prince Casimir—I know who she is. The problem is… she should have been dead for centuries now.”Yasha raised an eyebrow, “Explain what you mean by that.”Kaede’s ears twitched as she pulled an old worn book from her bag, sandwiched in between the pages of the notebook was an old photo, faded and scarred. “This is at least a hundred years old, which doesn’t make sense if she’s still alive. This notebook belonged to an alchemist and mage named Illyana. Look, it matches her perfectly.”Yasha took the picture, examining it closely. “She does resemble her, doesn’t she?” Yasha conceded, tapping her foot, “But that again might be just a coincidence. Do you know anything else about her? She casted two spells during that fight that we witnessed.”Kaede shook her head, “At the moment, I don’t have much more information. I know she’s a mage and a researcher. If it helps, I can give you a brief rundown of what I experienced in that dungeon later…when we have more time. It can narrow down our options.”Yasha nodded, as the two put on cloaks and headed out into the city to make their purchases for the journey ahead.

***With a new wagon and a team of sturdy horses, they departed through the north gate of Mothergreen as soon as possible to follow the northern road toward Madeline. A letter written by Kaede was left with the airship captain for delivery to the Angel's Sword Guild, detailing the events as they knew them. Each evening, as dusk settled, they camped off the main road to avoid drawing the attention of any lurking monsters or prying eyes. Aside from a distant growl that stirred their nerves one night, the journey passed without incident.

After a week of comfortable travel, the landscape began to change. Rolling fields of golden wheat stretched as far as the eye could see, dotted with quaint farmsteads along the road. The wooden blades of windmills turned slowly in the gentle breeze. Farmers paused in their work to wave at the passing travelers, unaware of the distinguished individuals hidden inside the wagon.

Soon, they reached the city gates of Madeline—structures built more for practicality than grandeur. After a brief exchange with the guards, who were visibly shocked upon recognizing them, the party made their way to the governor's mansion perched atop a hill at the heart of the city. They took a day to rest, the familiar surroundings offering a semblance of peace after their long journey.

The following day, they gathered in a simple conference room dominated by a long wooden table. Drinks and snacks were laid out, alongside strategic materials like maps and rosters spread for all to see. The atmosphere was a mix of anticipation and unease.

Fontina cleared her throat, drawing everyone's attention. "I've taken the liberty to do some research during our off day," she began. "It seems that Prince Casimir would like for a coronation ceremony to occur two months from now, after all investigations have concluded and people have had time to mourn. Obviously, the kingdom needs a ruler."

She paused, a hint of concern flickering in her eyes. "But something is bothering me a bit..."

Reaching into her satchel, she retrieved a rolled message and placed it on the table. "We have a report from Rin that a small army from Macaron marched to Éclair."

Rin—the commander of Madeline's saboteur and intelligence corps—was their most reliable source of information, the eyes and ears of Madeline.

Fontina continued, her tone worried. "I don't think they'd take such a brazen action, but this is quite worrisome. They could have sent us a long-distance message via magic if they had something to say to us. So either they have nothing to say, or it's too sensitive to send a message by magic."

Yasha's eyes scanned the map spread out on the table, even though she already knew the location of the city in question. "It's hard to imagine that army heading anywhere but toward us," she remarked. "How large is the force?" She picked up the report to examine it more closely.

"I don't like the sound of a small army marching in our direction, especially after everything that's happened this past week," Kaede added, her ears and tail drooping as she pondered the troubling news.

Helene frowned, tracing a finger along the map's routes. "It's been just over a week. That's hardly enough time to muster troops and resources, especially after a fiend attack," she noted. "Unless the logistics were already in place before the disaster."

"Does the report mention the army's position or departure date?" she asked, glancing up.

Yasha consulted the document. "They left Macaron about two days after the catastrophe, with an estimated force of 600 soldiers," she replied, placing the report back on the table. "It's unclear if they were prepared beforehand. They didn't leave immediately, and people did see us depart the city. I assume Casimir is using Macaron's forces rather than troops from his own lands."

Kaede shook her head, concern evident in her eyes. "Marching just two days after such a disaster suggests they had a preplanned objective," she pointed out. Turning to Fontina, she asked, "What do you think? Could they be planning to attack Madeline? I would have expected them to focus on rebuilding and reassuring their people first."

Fontina adjusted her glasses thoughtfully. "It doesn't make sense for them to attack us outright—it would be incredibly bold," she replied. "But why target us? Princess Cherry has no direct claim to the throne..."

"This timing feels suspicious," Lanes agreed, idly spinning a small throwing knife between his fingers. "Even if she doesn't have a claim now, she could in the future. If she gains popularity or support from other nobles, she could become a threat to Casimir. Eliminating potential rivals before they grow stronger is a common tactic."

Helene gazed grimly at the map, her eyes fixed on the marker indicating the Macaronian army in Éclair. "If the royal family has been decimated as the news suggested, then Princess Cherry might be next in line after Casimir," she said. "I can't think of any other immediate heirs. Fontina, am I overlooking someone?"

Fontina shook her head.

Limone raised her hand hesitantly. "But maybe they're not coming here, right? I mean, Éclair has great food!" She looked around, hoping for reassurance.

Kaede sighed softly. "While I appreciate the optimism, Limone, the timing and movement of an army can't be a coincidence," she said. Fidgeting with her tail, she added, "We need to be prepared in case this is essentially a declaration of war. We can't afford to be caught off guard."

"He might be attempting to eliminate Princess Cherry as a precaution," Yasha suggested. "Or perhaps he's afraid we'll accuse him of orchestrating the attack. Even if he's the rightful heir, losing public support could weaken his position."

Lanes stopped his fidgeting, his expression sharpening. "Another possibility is that Casimir intends to seize Princess Cherry under the guise of 'protecting' her after the recent events," he suggested. "He could use that as an excuse for moving such a large force—call it her protection escort."

Yasha turned to Fontina. "What troops do we have available? We should attempt diplomacy with the approaching army, but we must be ready for an attack."

Fontina unrolled a roster of their forces, laying it out on the table. "This is what we have," she said. "We could muster civilians to bolster our numbers, but I doubt that would sit well with the populace."

"No point in arming untrained civilians," Yasha agreed. "If it comes to that, we've likely already lost. Regardless of Casimir's stated intentions, unless he's willing to leave us be, we'll have no choice but to defend ourselves."

Just then, a soft knock sounded at the door. The head maid, Rina, entered gracefully, her hands delicately gathering her skirt as she offered a respectful bow. "Pardon the interruption," she said. "Commander Athena has a report from the city gate. An injured man, covered in dirt and... emitting quite a foul odor, is requesting an audience with Princess Cherry. He insists it's of utmost importance and that his message is for her ears only."

Yasha's eyes narrowed slightly. "He may have news regarding our current situation," she said looking for confirmation from the Princess.Cherry nodded with a serious expression, “Let us meet him then.”

Yasha immediately took command at Cherry’s order, "Keep him under close watch and escort him to the reception room. Ensure he's not a threat."  She trusted the head maid's capabilities, knowing that she did in fact double as another guard.

"Of course," the maid replied, departing promptly.

Helene moved to Princess Cherry's side, her posture protective yet poised. "We're ready whenever you are, Princess," she said softly.

As the door to the conference room closed with a thud behind them—the group prepared to meet the mysterious messenger, each member keenly aware that whatever news he bore could significantly impact their next moves.

***

They waited for the arrival of their sudden guest in the modestly appointed room, where practicality outweighed extravagance. Simple tapestries depicting local landscapes adorned the walls, their colors faded but still charming. A sturdy wooden desk stood at the far end, behind which Princess Cherry sat with a calm and composed demeanor. Flanking her were her guards, their uniforms neat yet unadorned, reflecting Madeline's frugal sensibilities.

The room's furnishings were functional: a plain wooden table surrounded by a few chairs, a shelf holding essential documents, and a small window covered by linen curtains that let in soft, natural light. A single vase held a few wilting flowers, adding a touch of modest beauty without excess. The polished hardwood floor showed signs of wear, testament to the room's frequent use.

After a few tense moments, the door swung open. The man stumbled inside, his appearance disheveled and marked by recent hardships. Blood smeared his once-pristine bandana, now muddied and torn, clinging to his forehead as a grim testament to his ordeal. Dark circles framed his weary eyes, and his clothes were ripped and stained, hinting at the battles he had endured. Collapsing to his knees, he looked up with hollow eyes, his voice barely above a whisper. "Uh... can I have some water, please?"

Recognition flickered across everyone's faces—this was Mizera Lezar, the King's closest friend. Princess Cherry regarded him with a mix of concern and determination, while the guards remained poised, ready to assist.

Yasha signaled to Rina. "Get him some water and find a healer," she ordered, stepping forward. "We owe you thanks for helping us escape during the attack. We might not have made it without you."

"You're a tough old bastard, that's for sure," Lanes added with a relieved chuckle. "Making it out of there alive is no small feat."

"Old man!" Kaede exclaimed, hurrying over as she rummaged through her bag. She produced a bottle of water and some rations, offering them to him. "I can provide some quick treatment while they fetch a healer," she suggested, glancing at Yasha.

Yasha nodded. "Go ahead."

Kaede began tending to his wounds with practiced care, her hands steady. Helene observed the scene quietly from beside Princess Cherry. Though they had only briefly encountered Mizera during the chaos, his timely intervention had been crucial. Still, Helene remained vigilant—it was her duty to protect the Princess, and caution was warranted, friend of the late King or not.

Mizera barely seemed to notice his wounds being tended to as he guzzled the provided water and practically inhaled the tough rations. He looked at Kaede appreciatively. "Thank you... The name's Mizera Lezar. I've been buddies with the King since his more... adventurous days."

[HEADING]On the topic of Fiends...
By: Chroma Lichtvog

Throughout my extensive life, I have always regarded Fiends at face value, as many in the world do. These creatures, for reasons both arcane and obscure, possess an insatiable hunger for the living, with a particular preference for those endowed with sentience. Yet, I find myself compelled to ask: Why can a Fiend masquerade as an ordinary object, seemingly devoid of metabolism? And why do they relentlessly pursue sentient beings as their prize?

In my centuries-long journey, I have endeavored to unravel these enigmas.

The ancient Lyrians, in their quest to categorize the myriad facets of existence, classified these entities into distinct groups:

Kratos: The mindless devourers.
Exousia: The lurking shadows of dread.
Principality: The harbingers of calamity.

The Kratos are the simplest of Fiends, those that linger at the periphery of dungeons—mindless husks driven solely by their primal urge to consume. In contrast, the Exousia represent the very essence of what we comprehend as Fiends: beings steeped in Astra, embodiments of malevolence that defy the laws of logic. Lastly, the Principalities remain elusive to me; I have yet to encounter them, but I envision them as the potent forces of ruin that dwell in the depths of the Astra Line.

From my confrontations with numerous Fiends, I have formed a hypothesis: these creatures are instinctively drawn to the soul cores of individuals. Those of us with powerful auras—myself included—find ourselves particularly enticing to them. Yet, beneath their predatory exterior lies an instinct for survival; perhaps what I perceive as fear is their acknowledgment of their own limitations.

I propose that Fiends feed not only on flesh but also on Astra and magical energy, evolving into increasingly formidable forms. With this evolution, they refine their tactics, becoming ever more adept at concealing their presence and acquiring prey.

I recall a pivotal encounter with a Fiend that exhibited signs of sentience, engaging in conversation. I naively hoped that understanding might bridge the chasm between us. However, my extensive research has led me to an undeniable conclusion: Fiends are beyond reason. They are devoid of morality, driven solely by an insatiable need to expand and replicate, regardless of the costs or deceptions required to placate us. They are the very embodiment of evil, and their only fate is to be vanquished.

The questions linger: From where do Fiends originate, and why do they exist? These mysteries haunt my thoughts, driving my pursuit for answers in a world where understanding often eludes us.
